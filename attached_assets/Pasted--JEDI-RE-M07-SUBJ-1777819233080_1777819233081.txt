================================================================================
PART I — M07 SUBJECT HISTORY ENGINE SPEC
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE & PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The subject property's own historical rent roll data is the highest-authority
non-override source on the platform. It captures the actual property under
the actual management in the actual submarket — no peer comp comes closer
than the asset itself.

Today the platform resolves traffic coefficients via a three-layer model:
    Baseline (M07 default) → Platform Peer Set (M07 calibrated) → User Override

This is incomplete. It treats peer-set output as the strongest non-override
signal even when subject historical data exists. The corrected hierarchy:

    Tier 4: Platform Default       (weakest)
    Tier 3: Peer Set (M07 calibrated to MSA/submarket/class/vintage)
    Tier 1: Subject Historical     (strongest non-override)
    Tier 0: User Override          (always wins)

Note: there is no Tier 2 in M07's resolution because, unlike Cashflow Agent's
underwriting tiers, M07 does not consume "owned other assets" data — that's
a Cashflow Agent feature. M07's subject signal is THIS deal's history only.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SUBJECT HISTORY TIER SYSTEM (S1–S4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Subject authority unlocks in stages as snapshot count grows. Each tier adds
new coefficients that were not computable at the prior tier.

S1 — Single snapshot (immediately on first upload)
    Every active lease's lease_start_date is a signing event. A single rent
    roll with 290 leases is effectively a 24-month signing history embedded
    in one document.

    Coefficients unlocked:
      • expiration_waterfall[24]            (from lease_end_date distribution)
      • current_state                       (occ, vacant, on-notice counts)
      • loss_to_lease_pct                   ((market − in_place) / market)
      • lease_term_distribution             (lease_end − lease_start histogram)
      • avg_lease_term
      • seasonality_observed_partial        (if 60+ leases give 12mo coverage)
      • signing_velocity_implied            (leases/month from start_date hist)
      • current_concessions_on_book
      • unit_type_breakdown_current

    Confidence: medium-high for state metrics, medium for seasonality if
    sample sufficient, low for forward-dynamics inference.

S2 — Two snapshots (diff extractor activates)
    The diff between snapshots reveals what *actually* happened in the gap.
    Implied dynamics become observed dynamics.

    Coefficients unlocked (via the diff extractor — see Part II):
      • actual_signing_velocity
      • actual_renewal_rate
      • actual_turnover_rate                (1 − renewal_rate)
      • trade_out_new                       (new lease vs prior tenant)
      • trade_out_renewal                   (renewal vs prior rent same tenant)
      • days_vacant_median
      • actual_seasonality_2pt              (begins to validate S1 implied curve)
      • concession_trend
      • rent_growth_realized

    Confidence scales with period_length × unit_count. Two snapshots six
    months apart on a 290-unit asset → ~65 signing/renewal events, which is
    statistically meaningful at the property level.

S3 — Four-plus snapshots (annual cycle coverage)
    Coefficients unlocked:
      • actual_seasonality[12]              (full validated curve)
      • concession_environment_trend        (quarterly dynamics)
      • pricing_velocity                    (rate of market-rent push)
      • eviction_skip_patterns              (unit-level identity tracking)
      • vacancy_persistence                 (specific units that sit)
      • unit_type_velocity_diff             (which floor plans lease faster)

S4 — Twelve-plus monthly snapshots
    Subject is self-calibrating. Peer set used only for:
      (a) Collision check (subject vs market divergence as signal)
      (b) Coefficients with insufficient subject sample (e.g., a new floor
          plan added mid-history)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. AUTHORITY HIERARCHY & BAYESIAN BLEND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Do not binary-switch from peer to subject when subject crosses a threshold.
Blend by sample weight, then check for collision.

    effective_value = w_subject × subject_value
                    + (1 − w_subject) × peer_value

    where:
      w_subject = min(1, n_subject_observations / n_required_for_full_confidence)

Per-coefficient n_required thresholds (codified table):

    Coefficient              | n_required full weight | Lower bound (peer floor)
    -------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------
    seasonality              | 24mo × 30 leases/mo    | 6mo gives w=0.25
    renewal_rate             | 60 expirations         | 20 gives w=0.33
    trade_out_new            | 30 new leases          | 10 gives w=0.33
    trade_out_renewal        | 30 renewals            | 10 gives w=0.33
    signing_velocity         | 12mo continuous        | 6mo gives w=0.50
    concession_environment   | 12mo observed          | 3mo gives w=0.25
    days_vacant_median       | 20 vacancy events      | 8 gives w=0.40
    loss_to_lease_pct        | 50 leases (S1)         | 20 gives w=0.40
    expiration_waterfall     | 1 snapshot suffices    | n/a (always w=1.0)

COLLISION RULE
    If |subject_value − peer_value| > 1.5 σ_peer, do NOT auto-blend silently.
    Surface in collision panel for user review. Material divergence between
    subject and peer is itself a SIGNAL — it indicates management quality,
    asset positioning, or segment difference. The user must decide whether
    that operational alpha persists into projection years.

4. DATA MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Three new tables.

CREATE TABLE rent_roll_snapshots (
    id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    deal_id         UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES deals(id),
    snapshot_date   DATE NOT NULL,            -- effective date OF the rent roll
    uploaded_at     TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),
    parser_source   TEXT NOT NULL,            -- 'yardi'|'realpage'|'onesite'|'entrata'|'manual'
    raw_document_id UUID REFERENCES documents(id),
    parsed_payload  JSONB NOT NULL,           -- normalized unit-level array (see §4.1)
    unit_count      INT NOT NULL,
    occupied_count  INT NOT NULL,
    UNIQUE(deal_id, snapshot_date)
);
CREATE INDEX idx_rr_deal_date ON rent_roll_snapshots(deal_id, snapshot_date DESC);

CREATE TABLE rent_roll_diffs (
    id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    deal_id         UUID NOT NULL,
    snapshot_a_id   UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES rent_roll_snapshots(id),  -- earlier
    snapshot_b_id   UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES rent_roll_snapshots(id),  -- later
    period_days     INT NOT NULL,
    events          JSONB NOT NULL,           -- per-unit transition events (see Part II §3)
    derived_metrics JSONB NOT NULL,           -- aggregated coefficients
    quality_flags   JSONB NOT NULL,           -- unresolved units, anomalies
    computed_at     TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW(),
    UNIQUE(snapshot_a_id, snapshot_b_id)
);

CREATE TABLE subject_traffic_history (
    deal_id                   UUID PRIMARY KEY REFERENCES deals(id),
    snapshot_count            INT NOT NULL,
    earliest_snapshot         DATE,
    latest_snapshot           DATE,
    coverage_months           NUMERIC,
    tier                      TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (tier IN ('S1','S2','S3','S4')),
    coefficients              JSONB NOT NULL,        -- resolved coefficient set
    confidence_per_coefficient JSONB NOT NULL,       -- 0–1 scalar per field
    peer_collisions           JSONB,                 -- material divergences
    last_rebuilt_at           TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);

interface Collision {
    coefficient: string;
    subject_value: number;
    peer_value: number;
    delta_sigma: number;            // how many σ_peer apart
    direction: 'subject_above' | 'subject_below';
    magnitude: 'minor' | 'material' | 'severe';   // <1.5σ, 1.5–2.5σ, >2.5σ
    narrative: string;              // human-readable explanation
}

9. CONCESSIONS BLOCK — WHERE SUBJECT HISTORY DOMINATES MOST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Concessions are the area where peer benchmarks are weakest (highly local,
time-sensitive, manager-discretionary). Subject history dominates here even
at S1.

    Concession line                | S1 source                          | S2+ source
    -------------------------------|------------------------------------|------------------------------------
    New Lease — Onetime            | concessions on leases signed L90D  | actual concessions on new-lease events
    New Lease — On-Going           | active rent abatements             | trended forward via concession_trend
    Renewal — Onetime              | rare in S1                         | renewal events with concessions
    Renewal — On-Going             | ongoing abatements active          | trended forward
    Average Concession per unit    | total / unit count active          | per-event average

Per-period values in the Projections grid (the $19K, $13K, $22K... numbers
in the screenshot reference) should be subject-derived if data exists, not
peer-imputed. Imputed concessions are a frequent source of model error.

10. EDGE CASES (codified handling required upfront)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

10.1 Unit renumbering across snapshots
     Yardi reorderings, combined units (2× 600sf → 1× 1200sf), split units.
     Identity-track via (sqft, position_id, original_unit_number_history),
     not just unit_number. The unit-identity resolver in Part II §2 handles
     this — combined and split units are classified as STRUCTURAL_CHANGE
     and excluded from market signal aggregates.

10.2 Renovation vacancy vs market vacancy
     Units intentionally held for renovation are not market signal. Need a
     flag from CapEx schedule (M22) or user tag. Renovation-down units are
     excluded from days_vacant_median, vacancy_persistence, and signing
     velocity numerators.

10.3 Tenant identity for renewal detection
     Tenant names unreliable across snapshots (truncation, formatting,
     "JOHN SMITH" vs "SMITH, JOHN" vs "Smith John Jr"). Use:
       • Primary: (unit_id, prior_lease_end_proximity_to_new_lease_start)
         Same unit, new lease starting within 30 days of prior end = renewal
         candidate
       • Corroborating: tenant name fuzzy match (Levenshtein < 3 OR token-set
         ratio > 0.85 on uppercased + punctuation-stripped names)
       • External tenant_id if available — overrides everything

10.4 Snapshot date proximity
     Two snapshots 14 days apart give little signal. min_period_days = 60.
     Below threshold, snapshots store but do NOT trigger diff extraction.
     Below 90 days, S3+ coefficients are not yet eligible for promotion.

10.5 Different parsers across snapshots
     Yardi snapshot followed by RealPage snapshot. Normalize to unified
     schema (§4.1) in parser_service before storage. Diff extractor operates
     on normalized payload only — never on raw parser output.

10.6 Lease-up assets
     Subject history early in lease-up is NOT representative of stabilized
     dynamics. The mode field in M07 (STABILIZED | LEASE_UP | REDEVELOPMENT)
     governs which subject coefficients are valid for trajectory projection.
     Lease-up signing velocity does NOT project forward into stabilized
     years — that's a category error. The aggregator must:
       • Tag coefficients with the mode they were observed under
       • The blend resolver excludes mode-mismatched subject coefficients
       • Trajectory projection uses peer or override only when subject is
         mode-mismatched

10.7 Concession field heterogeneity
     Some rent rolls track concessions inline, some in a separate sheet
     (Yardi often), some not at all. Parser must handle three cases.
     Aggregator must skip silently when concession data is absent — DO NOT
     impute zero. Imputing zero misleads downstream by suggesting "no
     concessions in market" when reality is "we don't have data".

10.8 Holdover tenants
     Tenant remains past lease_end_date on month-to-month. Both snapshots
     show same lease (unchanged start/end dates) but status updated to
     "MTM" or holdover flag. Classify as HOLDOVER — contributes to
     occupancy state but NOT to renewal_rate (no new lease was signed).

10.9 Eviction / skip
     Lease end date in B is earlier than lease end date in A for same
     tenant on same unit — premature termination. Classify as
     EVICTION_DETECTED. Days vacant calculated from premature_end_date
     forward. Eviction events do count toward turnover_rate (with category
     flag) but NOT toward standard renewal/new-lease arithmetic.

10.10 First-time upload with very small property
     20-unit property: even S2 may not give statistically meaningful
     renewal_rate. The n_required thresholds apply — when n_observations
     is below the lower bound, the coefficient stays at peer set with
     w_subject = 0 and is flagged in confidence_per_coefficient as 'low'.

       Actuals from post-close feed forward as additional snapshots.
       Subject history becomes a permanent learning loop, not just an
       underwriting input. This is the long-term defensibility play.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. ARCHITECTURAL RULE (codify in CLAUDE.md under M07 section)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SUBJECT-FIRST CALIBRATION

When subject property has historical observation, subject signals take
precedence over platform peer signals via subject_history source tier
(above peer_set:m07 in resolution order). Authority scales with snapshot
count and observation depth via per-coefficient sample thresholds. Peer
set serves as collision check, not primary signal, once subject sample
is sufficient.

Hierarchy: platform → peer_set:m07 → subject_history:sN → override

Tier ladder:
    S1 — single snapshot: state metrics + signing-history-derived coefficients
    S2 — two+ snapshots: observed dynamics (renewal, turnover, trade-out)
    S3 — four+ snapshots: validated annual cycles (full seasonality)
    S4 — twelve+ snapshots: subject is self-calibrating

Blend rule:
    effective = w_subject × subject + (1 − w_subject) × peer
    where w_subject = min(1, n_observations / n_required)

Collision rule:
    If |subject − peer| > 1.5σ_peer, surface for user review. Do not
    auto-blend silently. Material divergence is a SIGNAL.


================================================================================
PART II — DIFF EXTRACTOR ALGORITHM SPEC
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE & I/O
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The diff extractor compares two normalized rent roll snapshots (A earlier,
B later) and produces a structured event log + aggregated metrics. This
algorithm is the core engine that transforms two snapshots into S2 dynamics.

Input:
    snapshot_a: NormalizedRentRoll  (effective date T_a)
    snapshot_b: NormalizedRentRoll  (effective date T_b, where T_b > T_a)
    deal_meta:  { mode, total_units, building_count, unit_type_set }

Output:
    DiffResult {
      period_days: number,
      events: PerUnitEvent[],
      derived_metrics: AggregatedMetrics,
      quality_flags: QualityFlags
    }

Pre-conditions:
    • period_days ≥ 60 (else algorithm returns NO_DIFF result)
    • Both snapshots normalized to schema in Part I §4.1
    • Both snapshots from same deal_id

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. UNIT IDENTITY RESOLUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Match every unit in B back to a unit (or set of units) in A.

Resolution order, per unit in B:

Step 1: Exact unit_id match
    If snapshot_a.units contains a unit with same unit_id, confirm via:
        |sqft_a − sqft_b| / sqft_a ≤ 0.02     (2% tolerance)
        bedrooms_a == bedrooms_b
        bathrooms_a == bathrooms_b
    If confirmed → identity = MATCHED (1:1)
    If sqft/bed/bath mismatch → identity = AMBIGUOUS, fall through to Step 2

Step 2: Fingerprint match (handles unit renumbering)
    If exact unit_id not in A, search snapshot_a.units for:
        same building
        same floor (within ±1 floor)
        |sqft_a − sqft_b| / sqft_a ≤ 0.02
        bedrooms match
        bathrooms match
    If exactly one candidate → identity = MATCHED_RENUMBERED (1:1)
    If multiple candidates → identity = AMBIGUOUS_FINGERPRINT
    If zero candidates → fall through to Step 3

Step 3: Combined unit detection
    Search snapshot_a.units for two units satisfying:
        same building
        adjacent unit_ids OR adjacent positions
        sqft_a1 + sqft_a2 ≈ sqft_b (±2%)
        combined bedrooms ≈ b.bedrooms (one of A's units may have been
        kitchen/utility space converted)
    If found → identity = STRUCTURAL_CHANGE_COMBINED (2:1)
        Both source units flagged. New unit excluded from market signals
        for this period (the unit didn't exist in this form in A).

Step 4: New unit (lease-up addition or new construction phase delivery)
    If A's snapshot is from a lease-up phase and B has units not in A:
        Cross-reference against M22 CapEx / construction completion schedule.
        Classify as identity = NEW_DELIVERY.
        Excluded from renewal/turnover arithmetic but included in
        absorption tracking.

Step 5: Unresolved
    identity = UNRESOLVED with reason logged (sqft_outlier,
    multiple_fingerprint_matches, no_match_no_capex_record, etc.)
    Excluded from all aggregates. Counted in quality_flags.unresolved_count.

REVERSE PASS (units in A not in B):
    Run the same logic in reverse for any A unit with no B match. This
    catches:
        • Split units (1:2 — flagged STRUCTURAL_CHANGE_SPLIT)
        • Demolished units (rare, flagged UNIT_REMOVED)
        • Parser inconsistencies (flagged UNRESOLVED)

OUTPUT of identity resolution: a unit_pairs array of:
    { unit_b: UnitRecord, unit_a: UnitRecord | UnitRecord[] | null,
      identity: IdentityClass }

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PER-UNIT EVENT CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For each MATCHED or MATCHED_RENUMBERED unit pair, classify the event type
based on lease state in A vs B.

Let:
    A.lease = unit's lease in snapshot A (may be null if vacant)
    B.lease = unit's lease in snapshot B (may be null if vacant)
    T_a, T_b = snapshot effective dates

Event taxonomy:

EVT_NO_CHANGE
    Conditions:  A.lease and B.lease exist
                 A.lease.start_date == B.lease.start_date
                 A.tenant_normalized == B.tenant_normalized (or fuzzy match)
    Meaning:     Same lease throughout the period. Passive observation.
    Contributes: occupancy_persistence, in_place_rent at both endpoints

EVT_RENEWAL
    Conditions:  A.lease and B.lease exist
                 B.lease.start_date > A.lease.start_date
                 B.lease.start_date <= A.lease.end_date + 30 days
                 (tenant identity confirmed via §4 below)
    Meaning:     Same tenant signed a new lease at expiration.
    Contributes: renewal_rate numerator, trade_out_renewal,
                 lease_term_distribution

EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY
    Conditions:  B.lease exists; either A.lease is null OR
                 (A.lease exists with different tenant AND
                  A.lease.end_date < B.lease.start_date − 1 day)
    Meaning:     Unit went vacant, then leased to new tenant.
    Contributes: new_lease_count, days_vacant_median, trade_out_new (if
                 prior rent observable in A)

EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP
    Conditions:  A.lease and B.lease exist
                 different tenant
                 B.lease.start_date <= A.lease.end_date + 30 days
                 B.lease.start_date >= A.lease.end_date − 7 days
    Meaning:     Back-to-back turnover (tenant change with minimal vacancy)
    Contributes: turnover_rate, new_lease_count, trade_out_new

EVT_MOVED_OUT_VACANT
    Conditions:  A.lease exists, B.lease is null, B.status == VACANT_READY
    Meaning:     Tenant moved out, unit not yet re-leased as of T_b
    Contributes: turnover_rate (without renewal_rate offset),
                 days_vacant_running (lower bound)

EVT_MOVED_OUT_ON_NOTICE
    Conditions:  A.status == OCCUPIED, B.status == NOTICE
    Meaning:     Lease end approaching, tenant has given notice
    Contributes: forward turnover signal (12mo forward)

EVT_HOLDOVER
    Conditions:  A.lease and B.lease have identical start_date and end_date
                 B.status indicates month-to-month or holdover flag
                 T_b > A.lease.end_date
    Meaning:     Lease expired but tenant remains on MTM
    Contributes: occupancy_state only — NOT renewal_rate (no new lease)

EVT_EVICTION_DETECTED
    Conditions:  A.lease.end_date in A < lease_end_date implied by B
                 OR explicit early termination flag
                 (often: B shows tenant turnover before A.lease.end_date)
    Meaning:     Premature lease termination
    Contributes: eviction_rate, turnover_rate (with category flag)

EVT_RENOVATION_DOWN
    Conditions:  B.status == VACANT_DOWN AND B.renovation_flag == true
    Meaning:     Unit intentionally held for renovation
    Contributes: NOTHING to market signals. Tracked separately for CapEx
                 cross-validation against M22.

EVT_NEW_DELIVERY
    Conditions:  identity == NEW_DELIVERY (from §2 Step 4)
    Meaning:     Unit didn't exist in A; first appears in B
    Contributes: lease_up_absorption[period] only

EVT_STRUCTURAL_CHANGE
    Conditions:  identity == STRUCTURAL_CHANGE_COMBINED or _SPLIT
    Contributes: NOTHING to market signals. Logged in quality_flags.

4. TENANT IDENTITY RESOLUTION (for EVT_RENEWAL classification)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The renewal classifier requires confirming the same tenant signed both
leases. Tenant names in rent rolls are unreliable across systems and time.

Resolution order:

Layer 1: External tenant_id (if available)
    If both A.lease.tenant.tenant_id_external and B.lease.tenant.tenant_id_external
    exist and match → CONFIRMED
    If they mismatch → DENIED (definitively different tenants)

Layer 2: Fuzzy name match
    Normalize both names:
        • Uppercase
        • Strip punctuation
        • Strip suffixes (JR, SR, II, III)
        • Standardize whitespace
    Compute:
        • Levenshtein distance
        • Token-set ratio (handles "SMITH JOHN" vs "JOHN SMITH")
    Match if:
        Levenshtein <= 2 OR token_set_ratio >= 0.85
    → CONFIRMED if match, AMBIGUOUS otherwise

Layer 3: Co-incidence proximity (when name resolution is ambiguous)
    If new lease starts within 7 days of prior lease end on the same unit,
    treat as CONFIRMED renewal even with name ambiguity. This handles
    formatting differences ("JOHN R SMITH" → "SMITH, JOHN") that fuzzy
    match may miss.

Layer 4: Default to NEW_LEASE
    If Layer 1 denies OR Layer 2 fails AND Layer 3 doesn't apply,
    classify as EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY or EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP
    (depending on gap size).

Conservative bias: When in doubt, classify as new lease, not renewal.
Inflated renewal_rate is a worse model error than understated renewal_rate
because it leads to underestimating turnover costs and re-leasing risk.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PER-EVENT METRIC CALCULATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Trade-out (rent change on lease event)

    For EVT_RENEWAL:
        prior_rent = A.lease.current_rent
        new_rent   = B.lease.current_rent
        trade_out_renewal_pct = (new_rent − prior_rent) / prior_rent
        trade_out_renewal_dollars = new_rent − prior_rent

    For EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY or EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP:
        prior_rent = A.lease.current_rent  (the prior tenant's rent)
        new_rent   = B.lease.current_rent
        trade_out_new_pct = (new_rent − prior_rent) / prior_rent
        trade_out_new_dollars = new_rent − prior_rent

    Special case: EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY where A.lease is null
    (unit was already vacant in A): no trade-out computable. Skip from
    aggregate but track unit's market_rent_a vs current_rent_b for
    pricing_velocity signal.

5.2 Days vacant

    For EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY:
        prior_end = A.lease.end_date if A.lease exists else T_a
        new_start = B.lease.start_date
        days_vacant = (new_start − prior_end).days
        Floor at 0 (negative means lease overlap, treat as TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP).

    For EVT_MOVED_OUT_VACANT:
        prior_end = A.lease.end_date
        days_vacant_running = (T_b − prior_end).days
        // This is a LOWER BOUND — unit may still be vacant past T_b.
        // Tracked separately as "vacant_running" not "days_vacant_completed"

5.3 Lease term observed

    For EVT_RENEWAL or EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY or EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP:
        lease_term_months = (B.lease.end_date − B.lease.start_date).days / 30.44
        Round to nearest standard term (3, 6, 9, 12, 13, 14, 15, 18, 24).

5.4 Concession value

    From B.lease.concessions:
        onetime_value = onetime_remaining   (note: REMAINING, not ORIGINAL —
                                              if lease started 2 months before
                                              T_b and burn-off was 6 months,
                                              remaining = original × 4/6)
        ongoing_monthly = ongoing_monthly
        Total concession on lease (annualized):
            onetime + (ongoing_monthly × 12)

    Per-event new-lease concession aggregate excludes EVT_RENEWAL events
    (renewal concessions tracked separately).

5.5 Pricing velocity (S3+ only, requires 4+ snapshots)

    For each unit_type, fit linear regression of:
        market_rent observed over snapshot dates
    Slope (annualized) = pricing_velocity for that unit_type.
    Property-level pricing_velocity = unit-weighted average.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. AGGREGATION RULES (per-period derived_metrics)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

After event classification, aggregate across all events in the period.

6.1 Renewal & turnover rates

    expirations_in_period = count where A.lease.end_date in [T_a, T_b]
                            AND identity == MATCHED or MATCHED_RENUMBERED
                            AND NOT renovation_flag

    renewals = count(EVT_RENEWAL)
    new_leases_post_vacancy = count(EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY)
    turned_over_no_gap = count(EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP)
    moved_out_no_relet = count(EVT_MOVED_OUT_VACANT) where the moveout
                        is due to expiration in period

    actual_renewal_rate = renewals / expirations_in_period
    actual_turnover_rate = 1 − actual_renewal_rate
        (= (new_leases_post_vacancy + turned_over_no_gap + moved_out_no_relet)
           / expirations_in_period)

    Sample threshold: expirations_in_period >= 20 for any reportable rate.
    Below 20: report as null with confidence='insufficient_sample'.

6.2 Signing velocity

    total_signings = renewals + new_leases_post_vacancy + turned_over_no_gap
                     + new_deliveries
    actual_signing_velocity = total_signings / period_months
    period_months = period_days / 30.44

6.3 Trade-out aggregates

    Filter: exclude renovation_flag units, exclude null prior_rent.
    new_lease_trade_out_pct_mean = mean of trade_out_new_pct across new-lease events
    renewal_trade_out_pct_mean = mean of trade_out_renewal_pct
    Also compute medians and IQRs to support distribution-aware downstream use.

    Sample thresholds: >= 10 events for the metric to be reportable.

6.4 Days vacant

    Use only EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY events with COMPLETED days_vacant
    (where A.lease existed — i.e., we know when the prior tenant moved out).

    days_vacant_median = median of completed days_vacant values
    days_vacant_p75 = 75th percentile (for lender-style stress assumptions)

    Sample threshold: >= 8 events.

6.5 Concession aggregates

    new_lease_onetime_concession_mean = mean concession on EVT_NEW_LEASE_*
                                         and EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP events
    renewal_onetime_concession_mean = mean concession on EVT_RENEWAL events
    new_lease_ongoing_monthly_mean = mean of ongoing_monthly on new leases
    renewal_ongoing_monthly_mean = mean of ongoing_monthly on renewals

    Concession trend (S3+ only): compare current period mean against prior
    period mean. Direction = increasing | decreasing | stable.
    Magnitude = % change.

6.6 Seasonality (signings by month, requires sufficient coverage)

    Bucket all signing events by signing month (1–12).
    seasonality[m] = signings_in_month_m / mean_signings_per_month

    S2 partial seasonality: requires 12 months of period coverage AND
        >= 10 signings per month average.
    S3 full seasonality: requires 24+ months coverage across all snapshots.

6.7 Loss to lease

    For each occupied unit at T_b:
        ltl = (market_rent − current_rent) / market_rent  (clamped at 0 floor)
    loss_to_lease_pct = unit-weighted mean across occupied units.
    Excludes units with concessions actively burning off (those are
    naturally below market by design and skew the metric).

8. PSEUDOCODE — TOP LEVEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

function diff_extract(snapshot_a, snapshot_b, deal_meta):
    period_days = (snapshot_b.snapshot_date − snapshot_a.snapshot_date).days
    if period_days < 60:
        return DiffResult(period_days, [], {}, { min_period_satisfied: false })

    // Step 1: Identity resolution
    unit_pairs = []
    for unit_b in snapshot_b.units:
        match = resolve_identity(unit_b, snapshot_a, deal_meta)
        unit_pairs.append({ unit_b, unit_a: match.unit_a, identity: match.identity })

    // Reverse pass for A units not paired
    a_paired_ids = set of unit_a IDs already paired
    for unit_a in snapshot_a.units where unit_a.id not in a_paired_ids:
        reverse_match = resolve_reverse_identity(unit_a, snapshot_b, deal_meta)
        unit_pairs.append({ unit_b: null, unit_a: unit_a,
                            identity: reverse_match.identity })

    // Step 2: Event classification
    events = []
    for pair in unit_pairs:
        if pair.identity in [MATCHED, MATCHED_RENUMBERED]:
            event = classify_event(pair.unit_a, pair.unit_b, period_days)
        elif pair.identity == STRUCTURAL_CHANGE_COMBINED:
            event = EVT_STRUCTURAL_CHANGE
        elif pair.identity == NEW_DELIVERY:
            event = EVT_NEW_DELIVERY
        elif pair.identity == UNRESOLVED:
            event = null  // logged in quality_flags
        // ... other identity classes
        if event:
            events.append({ unit_id: pair.unit_b.id, type: event,
                            metrics: per_event_metrics(pair, event) })

    // Step 3: Aggregation
    derived_metrics = aggregate(events, period_days, deal_meta)

    // Step 4: Quality flags
    quality_flags = compute_quality_flags(unit_pairs, events, period_days)

    return DiffResult(period_days, events, derived_metrics, quality_flags)

9. WORKED EXAMPLES (edge cases that get this right)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Example 1 — Standard renewal
    A (2024-06-01): Unit 101, tenant SMITH JOHN, lease 2023-07-01 to 2024-06-30,
                    rent 1850
    B (2025-01-01): Unit 101, tenant SMITH JOHN, lease 2024-07-15 to 2025-07-14,
                    rent 1925
    Identity:   MATCHED (exact unit_id)
    Tenant:     CONFIRMED via Layer 2 (exact name match)
    Event:      EVT_RENEWAL (new start within 30 days of prior end)
    Metrics:    trade_out_renewal_pct = (1925 − 1850) / 1850 = 4.05%
                lease_term_observed = 12 months
                days_vacant = 0 (renewal, no vacancy by definition)
    Note:       The 15-day gap between expiration (6/30) and new lease start
                (7/15) is normal renewal lag — does NOT count as vacancy.

Example 2 — Renewal with name formatting differences
    A: Unit 204, tenant "JOHN R SMITH JR"
    B: Unit 204, tenant "SMITH, JOHN"
    Layer 1: no external tenant_id
    Layer 2: token-set ratio after normalization (strip JR, punctuation,
             standardize) ≈ 0.86 → CONFIRMED
    Event:      EVT_RENEWAL
    Note:       Without normalization, raw Levenshtein distance fails. The
                tokenization-based comparison handles this correctly.

Example 3 — Turnover with vacancy
    A (2024-06-01): Unit 305, tenant DOE JANE, lease 2023-04-01 to 2024-03-31
                    [holdover MTM; A also shows a notice flag]
    B (2025-01-01): Unit 305, tenant LEE MICHAEL, lease 2024-09-15 to 2025-09-14,
                    rent 1975
    Identity:   MATCHED
    Tenant:     DOE vs LEE — Layer 2 match fails (Levenshtein > 2,
                token_set_ratio ≈ 0.0)
    Event:      EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY
    Days vacant: A.lease.end_date was 2024-03-31, but A's snapshot also showed
                 holdover. Use A.lease.end_date as conservative prior_end.
                 days_vacant = (2024-09-15 − 2024-03-31) = 167 days
                 Caveat: 167 includes holdover period. The metric becomes
                 noisy if holdover is common. Recommended refinement: use
                 actual vacate_date if rent roll provides it (Yardi often
                 has a "moved_out_date" field separate from lease_end_date).

Example 4 — Combined unit
    A (2024-06-01): Unit 401 (sqft 600) and Unit 402 (sqft 600), both
                    occupied, two separate tenants
    B (2025-01-01): Unit 401A (sqft 1180), single occupied tenant
    Identity:   STRUCTURAL_CHANGE_COMBINED (sqft 401 + 402 = 1200, B sqft
                1180 within 2% tolerance)
    Event:      EVT_STRUCTURAL_CHANGE
    Treatment:  Excluded from rent_growth_realized, trade_out, days_vacant
                Cross-reference M22 CapEx schedule for combination project
                Reported in quality_flags.structural_changes_count

Example 5 — Renumbered unit (Yardi reorder)
    A (2024-06-01): Unit "B-101", building B, floor 1, sqft 875, 2/1.5
    B (2025-01-01): Unit "B101" (no hyphen), building B, floor 1, sqft 875, 2/1.5
                    (No exact unit_id match because of hyphen change)
    Step 1:     fails (id "B-101" not in B)
    Step 2:     fingerprint match on (building, floor, sqft, bed, bath)
                → exactly one candidate "B101"
    Identity:   MATCHED_RENUMBERED (1:1)
    Event:      classified normally based on lease state

Example 6 — Mid-period eviction
    A (2024-06-01): Unit 502, tenant ROBERTS, lease 2023-10-01 to 2024-09-30,
                    rent 2100
    B (2025-01-01): Unit 502, tenant CHEN, lease 2024-08-01 to 2025-07-31,
                    rent 2100
    Identity:   MATCHED
    Tenant:     ROBERTS vs CHEN — different
    Lease end in A: 2024-09-30; new lease start in B: 2024-08-01
                — new tenant started BEFORE prior tenant's scheduled end
                → Premature termination of ROBERTS' lease
    Event:      EVT_EVICTION_DETECTED (with subsequent re-lease to CHEN)
    Metrics:    Categorized as turnover (EVT_TURNED_OVER_NO_GAP semantics)
                with eviction flag. Counted in eviction_rate aggregate
                separately. Trade-out computed: (2100 − 2100) / 2100 = 0%
                (no rent change in this case — but might be different
                in real eviction scenarios)

Example 7 — Renovation unit excluded
    A (2024-06-01): Unit 110, status VACANT_DOWN, renovation_flag = true
    B (2025-01-01): Unit 110, tenant NGUYEN, lease 2024-11-15 to 2025-11-14,
                    rent 2300, renovation_flag = false
    Identity:   MATCHED
    Treatment:  EVT_NEW_LEASE_AFTER_VACANCY but FLAGGED as
                post-renovation lease. Days vacant calculated from a
                renovation-aware baseline (renovation completion date from
                M22 CapEx) NOT from when unit went down. If M22 data
                unavailable, exclude from days_vacant_median entirely.
                Trade-out = null (no comparable prior rent on a
                renovated unit).
                The new market_rent established here DOES feed
                pricing_velocity for the renovated cohort.

10. PERFORMANCE NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• A 300-unit property × 10 snapshots = 9 pairwise diffs (consecutive only).
  Do not compute all 45 unordered pairs — only consecutive in time order.
  Older diffs are not invalidated when newer snapshots arrive.

• Identity resolution is the hot path. Build an in-memory hash:
      key = (building, floor, sqft_bucketed_to_25sf, bedrooms, bathrooms)
      value = list of unit records
  Fingerprint lookup becomes O(1) per unit. Full resolution for 300 units
  runs in <50ms.

• Diff records are append-only. Once computed, never recomputed unless
  parser_source for a snapshot changes (re-parse). Diff records carry a
  parser_version field for invalidation tracking.

• Aggregation step over events for a single deal's full history (S3+):
  scan all events across all diffs for that deal. Cap at 50 snapshots per
  deal — beyond that, sliding window of most recent 24 months for forward
  signals, full history retained for archive.

11. TESTING STRATEGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Unit identity resolution: synthetic test set covering all 5 identity
  classes plus the 4 reverse-pass cases. Required pass rate: 100% on the
  test set.
• Event classification: matrix of (A.lease state, B.lease state, gap_days,
  tenant_match) with expected event type for every combination.
• Tenant fuzzy match: held-out set of 500 known-renewal cases and 500
  known-turnover cases from production data. Required: precision >= 0.95
  on renewal classification (low false positive rate is critical —
  inflated renewal_rate is the worst failure mode).
• Aggregation: regression tests against a fixture set of known-output
  diffs. Any change to thresholds or classification logic must produce
  diff against the fixture set, surfaced for review.
• End-to-end: parse a known Yardi rent roll set (3+ snapshots), verify
  S2 coefficients land in subject_traffic_history with expected values
  ±0.5%.
