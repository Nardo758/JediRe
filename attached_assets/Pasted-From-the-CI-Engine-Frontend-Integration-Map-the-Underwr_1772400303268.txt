From the CI Engine Frontend Integration Map, the Underwriting Archive was designated as one of the 3 genuinely new pages at route /archive with P2 priority. But the question of where it sits in the navigation is worth thinking through, because it serves two fundamentally different user intents.
The archive portion (Item 12 — save every deal underwritten) is portfolio/historical data. It's YOUR deals, YOUR assumptions, YOUR outcomes. That aligns naturally with the Assets Owned / Portfolio section of the app. The user mindset is "show me my history and what I've learned from it." So the Deal Archive tab and Assumption Benchmarks tab could live as new tabs on the Assets Owned page rather than a standalone page. You already have Assets Owned at /assets-owned with a grid view and performance/documents/grid tabs. Adding an "Archive" tab and a "Benchmarks" tab there keeps all portfolio intelligence in one place. The flow becomes: active owned assets (Performance & Rankings tab you just built in T008) → historical deals (Archive tab) → what the data taught you (Benchmarks tab). That's a natural user journey.
The property records intelligence portion (Item 14 — derive supply signals, distress signals, business opportunities from county records) is market-level intelligence, not portfolio-specific. It tells you about what's happening in a submarket regardless of whether you own anything there. That belongs in Market Intelligence. Specifically, the Property Records tab and Supply Signals tab map to two existing tabs that need enhancement: the Future Supply tab (which is already designed as the 10-year Supply Wave Analysis but disconnected from the Supply Agent) absorbs the supply signals from property records (plat recordings, impact fees, utility connections, CO filings, demolition permits). And the Active Owners tab (already in the Market Intelligence integration map as a P2 enhancement) absorbs the ownership chain data, foreclosure filings, code enforcement liens, and estate/probate transfers.
So the answer is: don't build a standalone /archive page. Split it:
The Deal Archive + Assumption Benchmarks → new tabs on /assets-owned. This is where users already go to manage their portfolio. Adding historical deal snapshots and benchmark analytics there creates a complete portfolio intelligence hub: what you own now, how it's performing, what you've underwritten in the past, and what you've learned.
The Property Records Intelligence + Supply Signals → enhance existing tabs on /market-intelligence. Supply-derived signals (permits, COs, land sales) feed into the Future Supply tab. Ownership/distress signals (foreclosures, liens, probate) feed into the Active Owners tab. Transaction velocity and 1031 exchange flows feed into the Trends tab.
This eliminates one of the three new pages entirely, which is cleaner. You end up with 2 new pages (Opportunity Center and Pattern Intelligence) instead of 3, and the data flywheel intelligence is embedded where users naturally encounter it rather than siloed in a separate destination they'd have to remember to visit.
