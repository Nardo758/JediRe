STOP. The "Food / Service / Location" review sentiment categories are WRONG. Those are Google Maps restaurant review sub-ratings. This is a commercial real estate platform.
The correct review sentiment categories come from our Google Reviews Intelligence spec (Pattern Recognition Engine PR-01). Property-level reviews are NLP-classified into these categories:
Review Sentiment rows (F5 / Traffic Intelligence section):

Overall Sentiment — aggregate score across all categories (-1 to +1)
Maintenance Sentiment — #1 value-add signal; high complaint rate + strong traffic position = operational fix cheaper than renovation
Management Sentiment — ownership transition detector; ±0.3 shift in 6 months signals new owner or neglect-for-sale
Location Sentiment — validates physical traffic position from FDOT/SpyFu data

These map to the property_reviews table schema: review_text, rating, date, nlp_categories, sentiment_scores. The backend service is googleReviewIntelligence.service.ts pulling from Google Places API, classifying via NLP into: Maintenance, Management, Amenities, Location, Noise, Parking, Pests, Safety, Value. The four rows above are the TOP-LEVEL rollups shown in the UI.
Do NOT map these to TrafficModule mi.* fields (overallAdjustment, demandFactor, digitalFactor, supplyFactor). Those are market adjustment coefficients for traffic prediction — completely different data. Review sentiment is a separate data source.
FDOT Traffic Counts rows — this part is correct. Year / Count / Source structure mapping to State DOT ADT historical data per station. Keep that as-is.
