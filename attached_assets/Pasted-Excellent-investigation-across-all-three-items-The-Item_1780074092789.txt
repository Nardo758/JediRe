Two scope additions to the canary deferral plan before locking 
the next steps:

ADDITION 1 — Expand ingest scope from "DeKalb+Fulton" to 
"primary metro Atlanta multifamily sale comp inventory"

DeKalb+Fulton solves the comp gap for these two specific S1 
subjects. But the underlying problem — geographic comp gaps 
causing methodology-divergence-as-data-gap — will recur for 
any Atlanta deal whose comps live in an un-ingested adjacent 
county. To prevent discovering the same problem deal-by-deal, 
the ingest should cover the primary metro Atlanta MF counties:
  - Core 5: Fulton, DeKalb, Cobb, Gwinnett, Clayton
  - Inner ring: Cherokee, Forsyth, Henry, Douglas, Fayette, 
    Paulding, Rockdale

Cobb is already ingested. The remaining 11 counties should 
ingest as one wave. Multifamily transaction volume concentrates 
in the inner counties; rural-Atlanta-MSA counties don't need 
priority.

This makes the canary deferral wait longer (more counties to 
ingest), but it produces a foundation that doesn't reveal the 
same 100bps divergence pattern for every Atlanta deal we touch 
in the next quarter.

ADDITION 2 — Date preservation through to time-series capability

Every sale comp ingested must preserve its sale_date through 
to the new schema's property_sales.sale_date field. Verify 
this for the existing Cobb County ingest and lock it as a 
requirement for the expanded metro ingest:

  1. Source rows have dates preserved when transformed into 
     property_sales rows
  2. synthesize-implied-cap-rates.ts produces per-period 
     aggregates (cap rate by quarter, PPU by year), not just 
     point-in-time medians
  3. The valuation comp reader supports as-of-date filtering 
     so a 2018 deal can be valued against 2018-and-prior 
     comps (the as-of-date discipline from the backtest 
     harness spec)

The architectural value: with full metro Atlanta + dates 
preserved + time-series synthesis, the platform has 
comp-anchored time-series for the metro. That unlocks:
  - Cap rate trajectory (compression vs expansion signals 
    for F4 Markets, Exit Timing tab)
  - Transaction velocity over time (M20 Exit Strategy timing)
  - As-of-date valuations (proper backtesting against ground 
    truth, when ground truth becomes available per Item 3)
  - Freshness indicators with real per-comp dates (per F-key 
    triage Wave A)

Without dates preserved, ingesting more counties produces 
more point-in-time inventory but doesn't unlock the time-series 
capabilities the platform's analytical surfaces depend on.
