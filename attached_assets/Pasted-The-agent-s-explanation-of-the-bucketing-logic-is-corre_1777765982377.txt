The agent's explanation of the bucketing logic is correct, but it's missing the actual problem in front of you. Look at what the bar is showing:
Every floor plan is 100% MTM. The TOTALS row reads 186 MTM, which exactly matches 186 occupied units (232 × 80.2%). Every single occupied lease in the building is being bucketed as month-to-month. That's not real — no stabilized property has 100% MTM. It's the same parser failure pattern we just diagnosed for the rents, surfacing in a different column.
Here's why: the bucketing logic falls into MTM on two conditions — actual MTM leases AND lease_expiration_date is null. The parser couldn't read the Lease Expiration column on this file (same reason it couldn't read the rent and charge columns), so every unit ships with a null expiration date, and the bucketer dumps every null into the MTM bin. The math is doing what it's told. The input is broken.
So your rent roll parser actually failed on more than just rents on the 464 Bishop file. It's missing rents, charge codes, AND lease expiration dates — i.e., everything that lives on the lease/charge side of the row structure. Unit-side fields (count, sqft, status, occupancy) parsed fine. That's exactly the signature of a standard parser running on an RRwLC file: the unit row reads cleanly, the nested lease-charge block is invisible to it.
Two things worth fixing on the platform, separate from re-uploading 464 Bishop with a cleaner export:
The MTM bucket is hiding parser failures. Null lease_expiration_date should not silently fall into MTM — that masks a data extraction failure as a (scary but plausible) operational signal. Add a sixth bucket — UNKNOWN or NO_DATA — and route nulls there. Render it as a hatched/striped grey segment so it's visually distinct from the real five-bucket curve. When UNKNOWN > 50% of occupied units, the extraction confidence flag should already have flagged the upload for review per your existing extraction_confidence schema.
The parser needs a per-column success guard. Right now it appears to silently degrade — extract whatever fields it can, leave the rest as nulls/zeros, and ship. For a rent roll, missing effective_rent across 100% of rows AND missing lease_expiration_date across 100% of rows should hard-fail the extraction with human_review_needed = true, not produce a partial render. The UI should show "Rent roll partially extracted — 4 of 7 critical columns mapped" rather than a clean-looking floor plan table with quietly bogus rents and a 100% MTM bar.
Both of these would have caught 464 Bishop at upload time instead of letting you discover it visually three screens deep.
