The key fix: Year 2 isn't computed independently from Assumptions. It's computed from Pro Forma Year 1 × Tier B trajectory rates.

Two-section Assumptions tab
Section A — Base Year (drives Pro Forma)
Document-sourced. These compute the Year 1 Pro Forma. All LayeredValue<T> with sources t12 / rent_roll / tax_bill / agent:cashflow / override.

Avg rent per unit, unit mix, count → from rent roll
In-place vacancy → from rent roll
OpEx by line item → from T12
Annual taxes → from tax bill (Florida: with reassessment-on-sale + 10% non-homestead cap baked in)
Insurance → T12 or platform benchmark
Year 1 debt terms → from term sheet or platform comp

Section B — Trajectory (drives Projections Y2+)
Platform/agent/user-sourced. Growth rates and timing.

Rent growth (flat % or per-year schedule: Y1=4%, Y2=3.5%, Y3+=3%)
OpEx growth %
Vacancy curve (ramp → stabilized, not flat)
Concession burn-off schedule
CapEx by year (lumpy, not percent — comes from the CapEx plan, not growth)
Refi triggers (when, at what rate)
Exit cap rate
Exit year


Computation flow
Pro Forma (Year 1 only, from Section A):
GPR_Y1     = avg_rent × units × 12
Vacancy_Y1 = GPR_Y1 × in_place_vacancy_rate
EGR_Y1     = GPR_Y1 − Vacancy − Concessions + Other Income
OpEx_Y1    = Σ T12 line items
NOI_Y1     = EGR_Y1 − OpEx_Y1 − Reserves
DS_Y1      = f(debt_terms)
CF_Y1      = NOI_Y1 − DS_Y1 − CapEx_Y1
DSCR_Y1    = NOI_Y1 / DS_Y1
Projections (Year 1 from Pro Forma; Years 2–N compounded):
GPR_Yn     = GPR_Y1   × (1 + rent_growth)^(n−1)     — or schedule lookup
Vacancy_Yn = GPR_Yn   × vacancy_curve[n]             — ramp, not flat
OpEx_Yn    = OpEx_Y1  × (1 + opex_growth)^(n−1)
CapEx_Yn   = capex_schedule[n]                       — lumpy, from plan
DS_Yn      = f(debt_schedule[n])                     — IO vs amort
NOI_Yn, CF_Yn, DSCR_Yn → downstream
Exit: Sale Price = NOI_{exit+1} / exit_cap, less costs and payoff → equity proceeds to Waterfall.

Implications this fixes

Dependency chain is real: Documents → Section A → Pro Forma → Projections (with Section B). Pro Forma must compute first; Projections is strictly downstream.
Edit propagation is asymmetric:

Editing Section A (base year) → recomputes Pro Forma → ripples through every year of Projections.
Editing Section B (trajectory) → Pro Forma unaffected; only Projections Y2+ moves.


Excel export rule: In the Projections sheet, the Year 1 column should be a direct cell reference to the Pro Forma sheet (=ProForma!C5), not a duplicated formula. Years 2+ build off that cell.
Audit story is cleaner: "Why is Y3 NOI $2.4M?" → traces to Y1 NOI on Pro Forma × OpEx growth × rent growth. Two clear sources, one compounding rule.
Aggressiveness scoring (M36) lands cleanly: Mahalanobis distance applies to Section B (trajectory), where the question "is this aggressive?" actually lives. Section A is anchored by documents — it's not aggressive or conservative, it's just what the property is.
