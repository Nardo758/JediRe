Two things before continuing:

ITEM 1 — Don't try the three suggested future sources yet

PT-61, ArcGIS Hub search, and Tyler MUNIS are good candidates 
but they're three separate engineering investments. Picking 
one without resolving the broader strategy means committing 
to county-by-county discovery as the structural approach.

The honest broader question: what's the sale comp acquisition 
strategy? Three options:

A. Continue per-county discovery (try PT-61 for DeKalb/Fulton, 
   keep finding sources for each new market). Engineering 
   effort scales with market expansion. Permanent gap risk.

B. State-level aggregation integration. PT-61 via GSCCCA is 
   the candidate for Georgia — one integration covers all 
   counties statewide. Need to verify programmatic access is 
   feasible. Same approach would need to be repeated per 
   state, but each state covers many counties.

C. Commercial aggregator (ATTOM, Reonomy, RCA). Paid data 
   service with broad coverage. Cost structure shifts to 
   subscription / per-record. Resolves coverage definitively.

D. Operator-upload-primary per P10 in CLAUDE.md. CoStar 
   uploads become the canonical sale comp inventory for deal 
   work. County data becomes enrichment. No engineering 
   needed for missing counties; operators fill gaps when 
   deals require it.

Different choices have different cost / time / operational 
profiles. Replit can execute against any of them; operator 
needs to make the strategic call.

a) Run Gwinnett re-ingest now. Endpoint exists; it just
