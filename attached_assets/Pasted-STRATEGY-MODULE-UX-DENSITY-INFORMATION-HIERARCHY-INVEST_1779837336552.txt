STRATEGY MODULE — UX DENSITY & INFORMATION HIERARCHY 
INVESTIGATION

CONTEXT
The Inventory & Complexity investigation (STRATEGY_MODULE_INVESTIGATION.md)
revealed that the Strategy Module is already evidence-heavy (76% 
evidence-backed display surface, 47% always-populated). The 
"too complicated to look at" complaint isn't about too many 
input fields — the module is appropriately evidence-rich.

The remaining hypothesis: the complexity is in visual density 
and information hierarchy. Operators are shown a lot of real 
information across many blocks, and the navigation/visual 
treatment may make it hard to find what matters.

This investigation maps the visual surface and proposes a 
hierarchy-based redesign, no field reduction.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 1 — VISUAL INVENTORY OF EACH SUB-TAB AND BLOCK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. For each of the three sub-tabs in StrategyArbitragePage 
   (M08 v2 ANALYSIS, SIGNAL MATRIX, CUSTOM SCREENS):
   - Map the visual hierarchy: which blocks are shown, in what 
     order, at what visual weight (size, position, prominence)
   - Identify how many distinct interactive elements exist 
     (buttons, dropdowns, toggles, hover triggers)
   - Estimate the "first-glance information count" — how many 
     pieces of information an operator must process when the 
     view first loads

B. For the M08 v2 ANALYSIS sub-tab specifically (the heart of 
   the module):
   - DetectionBanner: visual weight + space consumed
   - SubStrategyComparison: card count + visual density per card
   - EvidenceReportBlock: 4 blocks (A/B/C/D) + Expected Return 
     tile; document per-block density
   - CorrelationTimingPanel: Golden Chain + active alerts + 
     3 indicator lists
   - PlanDocument: 7 sections (entry, hold, valueCreation, 
     capitalSequencing, exit, monitoring, pivotConditions)
   - BuyerTargetingPanel: traffic quadrant + institutional 
     activity + buyer types
   - Coordinator Narrative

C. Per-block, document:
   - What the operator's likely first question is when seeing 
     this block ("What's my strategy?", "What's the gate?", 
     "What evidence supports this?")
   - Whether the block answers that question quickly or buries 
     it
   - Whether the block's purpose is clear from its visual 
     treatment alone

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 2 — INFORMATION HIERARCHY ANALYSIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Identify what an operator most needs to know about a deal's 
   strategy:
   - The recommended strategy (1-2 word answer)
   - The confidence in that recommendation (high/medium/low)
   - The headline evidence (1-3 facts that justify the strategy)
   - The financial summary (IRR, hold period)
   - The risks/concerns

B. Compare this to current visual hierarchy:
   - Are these answers immediately visible at the top of the 
     module?
   - Or are they buried inside expandable blocks?
   - How many clicks/scrolls before the operator has the 
     headline answer?

C. Identify "evidence depth tiers":
   - TIER 1 (headline): What strategy, how confident, 1-line why
   - TIER 2 (operator review): Sub-strategy comparison, metric 
     stack, plan entry
   - TIER 3 (analyst depth): Math trail, correlation alerts, 
     pivot conditions, monitoring items
   - TIER 4 (deep dive): Full evidence report, Signal Matrix tab

D. Map current blocks to tiers and identify mismatches:
   - Blocks that should be TIER 1 but are currently buried
   - Blocks that are visible at TIER 1 but belong at TIER 3+
   - Blocks at the right tier but with wrong visual weight

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 3 — DENSITY HOTSPOTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Identify visual density hotspots — blocks where too much 
   information competes for attention:
   - Block A (Metric Stack): N rows × M columns; assess
   - Block C (Comp Evidence): scatter + visualization + 
     selectionCriteria + comp table
   - PlanDocument: 7 sections in single panel
   - CorrelationTimingPanel: Golden Chain + alerts + 3 indicator 
     lists

B. For each hotspot, evaluate:
   - Is the density essential (every element does work)?
   - Or is it accidental (legacy accumulation of features)?
   - What's the minimum density that preserves the block's 
     purpose?

C. Identify simplification opportunities:
   - Collapsible sections that should default-collapsed
   - Inline summaries that could replace expanded blocks at 
     first glance
   - Side panels that could move from always-visible to 
     on-demand

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 4 — NAVIGATION AND FLOW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Document the operator's likely workflow within the module:
   - Land on page → first task → next task → exit
   - Is the visual flow aligned with the workflow?
   - Are there decision points that should be visible 
     immediately but aren't?

B. Sub-tab usage:
   - M08 v2 ANALYSIS is the primary view
   - SIGNAL MATRIX serves analyst depth
   - CUSTOM SCREENS serves screening workflow
   - Are operators ever confused about which sub-tab to use?
   - Could any of these be consolidated or relabeled?

C. The relationship to other modules:
   - Strategy Module exists alongside F9 (Pro Forma), F3 
     (Market), M22, etc.
   - Should the Strategy Module's headline answer be visible 
     from F9 (deal summary surface)?
   - Or is it appropriate that operators navigate to the 
     Strategy tab specifically?

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 5 — REDESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Based on Steps 1-4, recommend specific UX changes:

A. Information hierarchy restructure:
   - What goes in TIER 1 (always visible, top of view)
   - What goes in TIER 2 (visible but not headline)
   - What collapses to TIER 3 (expand-on-demand)
   - What moves to TIER 4 (separate sub-tab or deep view)

B. Density reduction:
   - Per hotspot, specific reduction approach
   - Default-collapsed vs always-visible decisions
   - Inline summary patterns

C. Visual treatment:
   - Use existing Bloomberg design tokens (T)
   - Specific color/weight/position changes
   - Maintain the dark Bloomberg aesthetic

D. Comparison to other modules:
   - How F9, F3, F7 handle their density
   - Patterns the Strategy Module should adopt for consistency
