Direction is strongly correct. This is the right structural move for three reasons that the proposal already gets right, plus a few refinements worth making explicit.
Why this is the right direction
1. The old Section 5 was mixing two different concerns. Engine internals (T-codes, walk-ins, capture rates) sat next to user assumptions (occupancy targets, concessions). Anyone outside the platform team had no business seeing T-01. The replacement separates engine-internal calibration from user-facing assumption editing — that's exactly the right boundary.
2. The mapping table you produced is migration-grade. You've explicitly said where every old field goes. That's what makes a refactor safe: nobody can claim a field "got lost in the move" because the table proves otherwise. Keep this table; it becomes part of the migration documentation.
3. Sub-tabs match the F-key navigation pattern. F9 → Assumptions → [General | Leasing] is consistent with the Bloomberg-style deep navigation already in use. Two-level hierarchy is the right depth — three levels would be too much.
Refinements worth making explicit
The summary card on General — keep it lean and read-only
Recommend Option A from this menu (the simplest):

Read-only display of 4 metrics with source/confidence badges (consistent with platform pattern)
Click any metric → opens drilldown modal showing peer/subject/effective layers (read-only)
Modal has [→ Edit in Leasing tab] button as the only edit action

Do NOT make the cells on the summary card directly editable. That would create a third editing surface for the same LayeredValues (Global Leasing tab + Inline Blocks above Projections sections + summary card on General). Three editing surfaces for the same data is where consistency bugs live. The summary card's job is context and navigation, not editing.
For the four metrics on the card, recommend:

Current occupancy (with [SUBJ] / [PEER] / [PLAT] badge)
Target stabilized occupancy
Blended rent growth
Concession strategy (read-only display of MARKET / CONSERVATIVE / AGGRESSIVE enum)

Cost treatment is intentionally NOT on the summary card. It already lives in two locations (Deal Settings + F9 top bar) per the UI spec §6 — adding it to the summary card violates the dual-location-only principle.

The litmus test: "Does this assumption affect leasing velocity, occupancy, or rent capture?" If yes → Leasing tab. If no → General tab. Bad debt is borderline (could go either way) — recommend Leasing because it's revenue-side and the M22 actuals path will calibrate it through the same pipeline as renewal rate.
Renovation fields — separate concern, not General-tab residual
Renovation Traffic Lift % and Target After-Repair Rent are V2-mode fields (VALUE_ADD_REPOSITIONING). They don't belong in General OR Leasing in V1 because the value-add mode isn't shipped yet. Three options:

Option A: keep them in their own collapsible "Renovation" section on the Leasing tab, hidden until mode = VALUE_ADD or REDEVELOPMENT (mode-conditional, same pattern as Lease-Up Strategy / Recovery Strategy categories E and F)
Option B: ship them temporarily on General with a "VALUE_ADD MODE — V2" banner, migrate to Leasing when V2 lands
Option C: strip them entirely until V2 lands; users who need value-add modeling today use overrides

Recommend Option A — they're already mode-conditional in nature, the Leasing tab is the right home, and the same traffic.mode.effective subscription that hides Lease-Up Strategy in stabilized deals will hide Renovation in non-value-add deals.

One thing worth thinking about now to avoid future rework
Sub-tab proliferation. If [General | Leasing] is right today, what's the stopping rule for tomorrow? Likely candidates for future sub-tabs:

Debt (when capital structure complexity grows — refinancing scenarios, mezz, agency)
Capital (S&U, waterfall, returns) — though these probably belong on F9 sibling tabs, not under Assumptions
Tax (when FL-specific complexity expands to other states)

Recommend stopping at three sub-tabs maximum under Assumptions. Beyond three, navigation becomes unwieldy. If a fourth concern emerges, it probably belongs on its own F-key page rather than as a sub-tab. This is a discipline question — codify it now, before the temptation to add a fourth tab arrives.
