Here's my analysis. There are several significant issues with the zoning interpretation shown in your Dev Capacity tab.
First — this site is already developed. The Edley, a 392-unit apartment complex built by Fairfield Residential, is already constructed at 1950 Piedmont Circle on this 4.8-acre parcel. Urbanize Atlanta The project includes buildings standing four to six stories with studios through two-bedroom units. Urbanize Atlanta If you're running a hypothetical analysis, that's fine, but worth flagging.
Now to the zoning interpretation issues:
1. Base Zoning is Confirmed MRC-2-C — The underlying zoning designation is correct. Neighboring parcels on Piedmont Circle are confirmed as zoned MRC-2-C. Atlantaleasing
2. FAR of 4.00 for By-Right is too high. Per Atlanta's Table A, MRC-2 has a nonresidential FAR of 2.5, residential FAR of 1.49, for a combined maximum of 3.196. Elaws The open space bonus allows residential floor area to be calculated on gross lot area (instead of net), which could push the effective residential component slightly higher, but a combined by-right FAR of 4.00 doesn't match the ordinance. The correct by-right combined FAR is 3.196, not 4.00.
3. The BeltLine Overlay column is fundamentally wrong. This is the biggest issue. The City of Atlanta's own materials explicitly state the BeltLine Overlay does NOT change existing underlying zoning, does NOT change densities or permitted uses — density and use remain governed by underlying zoning. City of Atlanta The platform is treating it as a separate development path with its own FAR (3.00) and density (36.0 units/acre), which contradicts how the overlay works. In reality, the BeltLine Overlay only adds form-based design requirements — streetscape standards, supplemental zones, façade activation, building-to-street relationships, landscaping, and similar controls. The FAR and density remain MRC-2-C.
4. Parking is inverted. In February 2024, Atlanta City Council approved an ordinance eliminating minimum parking requirements within the BeltLine Overlay District Rough Draft Atlanta for most uses except certain food/alcohol establishments. Yet your platform shows 593 required spaces for the BeltLine column and "0 (exempt)" for By-Right. This is backwards — the BeltLine Overlay is specifically where parking was eliminated, while standard MRC-2 still has parking ratios (MRC-2 requires one parking space per 300 square feet of commercial floor area Elaws).
5. MRC-3 FAR of 3.20 is only the residential component. MRC-3's full combined FAR is 7.20 (4.0 nonresidential + 3.2 residential). Elaws Showing 3.20 understates the actual development capacity of a rezone to MRC-3 by roughly half.
6. Density in units/acre doesn't exist in the MRC code. Atlanta's MRC districts are FAR-controlled, not density-capped by units/acre. The numbers shown (65.0, 78.0, 36.0, 150.0) don't correspond to anything in the ordinance. Max units are a derivative of FAR ÷ average unit size, not a standalone control.
7. The "-C" conditional zoning matters. MRC-2-C means City Council attached specific conditions when this zoning was enacted. Those conditions could restrict height, FAR, use, or other parameters below the base MRC-2 maximums. Without pulling the specific conditional ordinance, the base MRC-2 numbers may overstate what's actually permitted here.
