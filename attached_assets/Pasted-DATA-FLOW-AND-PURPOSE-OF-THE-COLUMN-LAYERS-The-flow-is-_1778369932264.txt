DATA FLOW AND PURPOSE OF THE COLUMN LAYERS

The flow is:

  Pro Forma  →  Assumptions  →  Year 1  →  Projections

  - Pro Forma:    AS-IS source view, columns rendered live from each 
                  extraction source (broker_claims, T-12, rent roll, 
                  tax bill, platform benchmark)
  - Assumptions:  forward-looking decision layer; underwriter reviews 
                  source values, sets overrides; resolves to Year 1
  - Year 1:       deal_assumptions.year1 JSONB; the resolved 
                  assumption set Projections consumes
  - Projections:  multi-year cash flow forecast built from Year 1

The om/t12/rent_roll/platform/override slots are decision-support 
viewpoints, not just storage:

  Broker (om)    what the seller is claiming
  T-12           what the actual trailing 12 shows
  Rent Roll      what current in-place reality shows
  Platform       what market benchmarks predict
  Override       what the underwriter believes
  Resolved       the value Projections uses

These viewpoints surface on TWO surfaces, not one:

  - Pro Forma tab — live comparison columns; underwriter scans for 
    broker puffing, market dislocation, in-place degradation
  - Assumptions tab — source layers preserved in the year1 snapshot 
    alongside the override field; resolved value flows to Projections

WHY THIS FIX MATTERS

When broker_claims is clobbered by the data-router write race, BOTH 
surfaces lose the broker viewpoint at once:
  - Pro Forma reads live broker_claims, shows "—" in Broker column
  - Seeder reads the same clobbered broker_claims, writes null to 
    year1.*.om
  - Underwriter loses a comparison axis on Pro Forma and a source 
    layer on the Assumptions snapshot

Acceptable solutions preserve broker_claims through the data-router 
write sequence so both the live Pro Forma read and the seeder's read 
get the data extracted by the OM parser. Solutions that hide the 
column when null, fall back silently, or "simplify away" the slot 
are not acceptable — they mask a degraded decision-support state 
rather than fixing it.
