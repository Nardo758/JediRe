The #902 ticket as written doesn't match what was intended. Two 
specific corrections:

1. The recipient surface should be the full F1-F11 module set, 
not the current 6-section layout. The recipient gets the entire 
platform's analytical surface with the deal preloaded — same 
modules a sender sees, just accessed via recipient access token.

2. Interactivity should be the default state, not opt-in via a 
"My View" toggle. The recipient lands on the full F-modules with 
sender's values as the starting state, and can edit anything 
immediately. There's no "viewing mode" vs "editing mode" — there's 
just "the workspace, with this deal preloaded, with sender's 
configuration as the initial state."

The overlay pattern is correct (session-scoped writes, don't 
persist to sender's deal). The visual distinction for modified 
fields is correct (subtle indicator, sender's original value on 
hover, per-field reset). The architecture of source + sender 
snapshot + recipient overlay is correct.

What needs to change:
- The starting state of the recipient view is fully interactive, 
  not read-only
- There's no toggle between "sender's view" and "my view" — 
  there's just "the workspace" with overlay indicators showing 
  what the recipient has modified
- A "reset to sender's value" affordance per field, per module, 
  and globally — same as currently planned, just framed as 
  "reset modifications" rather than "switch to sender's view 
  mode"
- The recipient surface needs to expand from the current 6 
  sections to the full F1-F11 module set (this is the larger 
  piece of the rework)

If expanding to F1-F11 is too much for this single task, scope 
it as two phases:
- Phase A: fix the interactivity model on the current 6-section 
  surface (remove the "view mode" toggle, make editing the 
  default behavior, use overlay indicators instead of view 
  switching)
- Phase B: expand the surface from 6 sections to full F1-F11 
  modules

Both phases ship. Just be honest about which phase the work is 
in rather than landing on the wrong interpretation of the spec.
