Good question — the correlations serve two roles: they're both a visible analytical tool users interact with AND an invisible data feed powering widgets across the platform. Most of them don't need their own page. They embed into what you've already built.
Here's the exact mapping:
T002 (Trends Tab) — already built, this is the primary home for 5 correlations:
COR-01 (Traffic Surge vs Rent Growth), COR-04 (Wage Growth vs Rent Growth), and COR-13 (Rent-to-Income vs Rent Growth) are exactly what T002's RentTrafficWageChart and AffordabilityCeilingGauge display. COR-02 (Search Momentum vs AADT Growth) and COR-03 (Search Momentum vs Rent Growth) are the same chart family — digital leading indicators overlaid against financial outcomes. These five are the core of what users see when they open the Trends tab. The correlation r-values, divergence alerts, and affordability gauge you already spec'd are literally COR-01 through COR-05 and COR-13 rendered as charts.
T004 (Traffic Intelligence Section on Deal Page) — 4 correlations power the widgets:
COR-05 (Traffic Surge vs Vacancy) drives the trajectory arrow and walk-in prediction confidence. COR-15 (Sentiment Trend vs Lease Velocity) powers the Google Reviews sentiment section — the connection between improving reviews and faster leasing. COR-16 (Digital Share vs Occupancy) feeds the SubmarketRankPosition bar by showing whether this property's digital position predicts its occupancy trend. COR-17 (Traffic Velocity vs RevPAU) contextualizes the T-07 trajectory — is gaining traffic position actually translating to revenue? These don't render as standalone correlation charts. They render as the confidence indicators, trend arrows, and insight callouts within the widgets you already built.
T005 (Competitive Position Section) — 2 correlations drive the comp analysis:
COR-14 (Google Rating vs Rent Premium) is exactly what the AmenityGapMatrix quantifies — the dollar value of each quality improvement. COR-09 (Deliveries vs Concession Intensity) powers the PatternAlertCards when a delivery wave is hitting the trade area. Again, these don't show as "correlation charts." They show as the insight text: "Properties rated 4.3+ stars in this trade area charge $125/unit premium over 3.8-star properties."
T006 (Strategy Arbitrage Traffic Gates) — the gates themselves ARE correlation thresholds:
The traffic qualification logic (BTS needs ADT >15K + digital momentum >+15%, Flip needs T-04 Hidden Gem + TAR >1.15, etc.) comes from the Traffic Intelligence Opportunity Framework's strategy-specific thresholds, which were derived from the correlation relationships. COR-01's lead/lag timing is why BTS requires forward-looking digital momentum — you're building for 18-24 months out.
T003 (Deals Tab) — 1 correlation drives the Target Score:
COR-20 (Digital-Physical Divergence vs Price/Unit) is what makes a property show up as a high Target Score. Properties where digital demand has surged ahead of physical traffic and prices haven't caught up yet score highest.
The Pattern Intelligence page (Phase 2, /patterns) — this is where correlations become VISIBLE as a tool:
The Correlation Lab tab you spec'd in Phase 2 is where users can actually see the scatter plots, select X and Y axes, adjust time lags, and explore relationships themselves. That's the only place where correlations are rendered AS correlations. Everywhere else, they're invisible — they power the insights and scores that users see.
Backend architecture — one service, many consumers:
All 21 correlations compute in a single correlationEngine.service.ts that runs as a monthly background job. It writes results to market_correlations table (submarket, metric_x, metric_y, r_value, lag_months, divergence_flag, timestamp). Every frontend component that needs a correlation result just reads from that table — the Trends tab reads the r-values for display, the Deal Page reads divergence flags for alerts, the Strategy gates read threshold evaluations.
So the answer: you don't need a new page or section for correlations in Phase 1. They're already embedded in T002 through T006. The visible "Correlation Lab" where users explore them directly is a Phase 2 item on the Pattern Intelligence page. For the signals build, the work is the backend service that computes the correlations and the API routes that serve them to the components you've already built.
