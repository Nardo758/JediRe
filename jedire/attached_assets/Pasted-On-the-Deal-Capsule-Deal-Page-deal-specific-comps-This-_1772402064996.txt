On the Deal Capsule / Deal Page — deal-specific comps:
This is T005 from the build spec, the Competitive Position Section. It answers "how does THIS property stack up against its specific competitors?" The dual tabs (Trade Area Comps and Like-Kind Comps) are bound to a specific deal — the trade area comp set is defined by THIS property's trade area boundary, the like-kind set is matched to THIS property's vantage group (class, vintage, size). The Amenity Gap Matrix shows what THIS property is missing vs its local comps. The rent position scatter plots THIS property against its neighbors. Every data point on this section has the subject property as the anchor.
This belongs inside the Deal Capsule's Layer 2 (Platform Intelligence) specifically. The 3-layer architecture is broker data vs platform intelligence vs user adjustments. Comp analysis is quintessential platform intelligence — the platform pulls the comp set, calculates the rent premium/discount, identifies the amenity gaps, and detects patterns like vintage cascading or amenity arms races. The broker might include their own comp set in Layer 1, and then the collision analysis shows where the broker's comps diverge from the platform's comps. That's exactly the kind of insight the 3-layer architecture was designed for: "Broker says 5 comps with avg rent $1,720. Platform identified 12 comps with avg rent $1,685. Broker cherry-picked the higher-rent comps."
So within the Deal Capsule, comp analysis sits in Layer 2 as a section or tab alongside the other platform intelligence (traffic scores, supply pipeline, demand signals). When the user expands into the full Deal Page Enhanced, it becomes the standalone Competitive Position Section (T005) with the full dual-tab experience.
On the Control Panel sidebar — market-level comps:
The sidebar serves a different purpose. It's not anchored to a specific deal — it's showing competitive intelligence for a submarket or trade area as a whole. The comp data that flows here is aggregate: "In the Midtown submarket, here are the top 20 properties ranked by PCS. Here's how they cluster by vantage group. Here's where the rent ceiling sits. Here's who's gaining and losing digital share."
This is the Power Rankings view (T001) and the market-level competitive patterns. The sidebar should show the competitive landscape that ANY deal in that submarket would face. When a user selects a specific deal from the sidebar, THEN the view shifts to deal-specific comps (navigating to the Deal Capsule/Deal Page).
The flow is: sidebar shows "Midtown submarket has 41 tracked properties, top performer is X at PCS 94, rent ceiling is $2,150, 3 Hidden Gems detected" → user clicks a Hidden Gem → opens Deal Capsule with deal-specific comp analysis showing how that property compares to its 12 nearest competitors.
The key distinction:
The sidebar comp view answers "what does the competitive landscape look like in this market?" — it helps you pick WHICH deals to investigate. The Deal Capsule comp view answers "how does THIS specific deal compare to its competitors?" — it helps you underwrite the deal you've already selected. Same underlying data (PCS rankings, rent comps, amenity sets, traffic share), but filtered and presented differently based on whether you're browsing the market or analyzing a deal.
So both are correct — the comp analysis lives in both places, just scoped differently. The sidebar gets the market-level competitive overview with rankings and pattern detection. The Deal Capsule gets the property-specific comp grid, amenity gap matrix, and rent positioning analysis anchored to the subject property.
