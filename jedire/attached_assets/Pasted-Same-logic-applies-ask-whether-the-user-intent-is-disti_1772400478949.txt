Same logic applies — ask whether the user intent is distinct enough to justify a standalone page, or whether it embeds better into existing destinations.
The Rank-Me Tool (S5, Items 7-8) doesn't need its own page. It's a tool you use IN CONTEXT of a specific property. You never open the Rank-Me tool in the abstract — you always open it with a property in mind: "I'm buying THIS deal, where will it rank?" or "I own THIS asset, how do I get it to #3?" That means it lives in two places:
On the Deal Page — as the new section you already have in the integration map (after Strategy Arbitrage, before Financial). When a user is underwriting a deal, they click into Rank-Me, select a target rank, see the gap waterfall, get the prescriptive action plan, and see how it changes the ProForma. This is the primary home. The user flow is: evaluate deal → see its current/projected rank → set a target → get the renovation/management plan → see the financial impact → make the bid. It's a linear workflow that belongs inside the deal page, not on a separate page.
On Assets Owned — as the quick-access button you already spec'd in T008 (Performance & Rankings tab). For owned assets, the user clicks "Rank Me" on a specific property and it opens the same tool, just prefilled with that asset's current data and actuals instead of underwriting projections. This could be a slide-out drawer or modal rather than navigating away from the portfolio view. The user shouldn't lose context of their portfolio to use the tool on one asset.
The Competitive Intelligence sidebar page you already built has a "Projection" section — that's the right conceptual home for the Rank-Me logic, but it should be accessed FROM deal pages and asset pages, not navigated to independently. Think of it like Google Maps directions — you don't go to a "Directions Page," you click "get directions" from wherever you are.
The Pattern Intelligence page is the one that genuinely needs to be its own page, but it can be smaller than originally scoped. Here's why it's different from the others:
The Pattern Engine's unique value is serendipity — showing users patterns they didn't ask for and wouldn't have found by looking at any single deal or market. "Rent growth in your 3 tracked submarkets is diverging from wage growth in a way that historically precedes a 6-month rent deceleration." "Business formations in healthcare NAICS near your Jensen Beach asset spiked 40% — that's the same pattern that preceded the 2023 rent acceleration in Stuart." These cross-cutting insights don't belong on any one deal page or any one market tab because they span multiple deals and multiple markets simultaneously.
However, most of the Pattern Engine's individual components already have homes. Google Review Intelligence lives on the Deal Page (T004 sentiment analysis) and could live on a Market Intelligence tab for market-wide review analysis. The Correlation Lab lives on the Trends tab (T002 already shows the rent-traffic-wage charts). Business Formation Clusters live on the Market Intelligence Business Clusters tab (already in the integration map as P3).
So what's left for the standalone Pattern Intelligence page is really just the Detected Patterns feed — the unified stream of "here's what the system found across all your tracked markets." That's more of a notification/insight feed than a full analytical page. Which means it could become a section on the Dashboard rather than its own page. The Dashboard currently has: map, deal cards sidebar, drawing tools. The integration map already adds an Opportunity Alert Feed widget to the dashboard. The Pattern Detection feed is conceptually the same thing — intelligence the platform pushes to you. Merge them:
Dashboard gets an "Intelligence Feed" that combines opportunity alerts (S6) with pattern detections (S4). One feed, two types of cards. Alert cards say "here's a deal to look at and what strategy to use." Pattern cards say "here's a relationship we detected that affects your portfolio/markets." The user opens the dashboard, sees their map and deals on the left, and the intelligence feed on the right. Click an alert → goes to the deal. Click a pattern → goes to the relevant Trends tab or Market Intelligence page with that correlation highlighted.
The Correlation Lab (interactive exploration where users pick X and Y axes) becomes a feature within the Trends tab — an "Explore Correlations" mode toggle that lets users switch from the default rent-traffic-wage view to a custom variable selector. Power users love this; casual users never need it. Embedding it in Trends rather than a separate page means users discover it naturally while looking at market trends.
So the final architecture becomes:
Zero new standalone pages for these two features. The Rank-Me tool embeds as a section/drawer on Deal Page and Assets Owned. The Pattern Detection feed merges with the Opportunity Alert Feed on the Dashboard. The Correlation Lab becomes an advanced mode on the Trends tab. Individual pattern components (reviews, business formations) become tabs on Market Intelligence.
That brings the total new pages down to 1 — just the Opportunity Center (/opportunities), which is the only feature with a truly distinct user intent: "show me deals I don't own yet that the platform thinks I should look at." Everything else embeds into existing pages where users already go.
