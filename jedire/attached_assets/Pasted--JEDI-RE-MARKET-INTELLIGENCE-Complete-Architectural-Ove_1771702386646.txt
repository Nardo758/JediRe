# PART 6: KEY DESIGN PRINCIPLES

## 6.1 Progressive Disclosure
- **Overview**: 6 numbers + 5 bars + AI summary = 30 seconds
- **Market Data**: Full research library = 5-15 minutes
- **Property Flyout**: Every data point for one property = deep dive
- **Never force a user to see more than they need**

## 6.2 Data Attribution
- Every number shows source on hover: `"D-01 | Source: BLS + Municipal | Updated: Feb 15, 2026 | Confidence: HIGH"`
- Color language: Green = Demand, Red = Supply, Orange = Momentum, Purple = Position, Gray = Risk, Teal = Composite, Blue = Traffic, Violet = Dev Capacity

## 6.3 Vertical Goes Deep, Horizontal Goes Wide
- My Markets tabs = everything about ONE market
- Compare / Owners / Supply = questions that span MULTIPLE markets
- A user should never need to switch between single-market tabs to answer a cross-market question

## 6.4 The Traffic Engine Is Property-Level
- T-outputs appear in flyouts, deal cards, and opportunity rankings
- They do NOT appear as market-level aggregates (unlike D-outputs)
- Exception: T-02 and T-03 can be averaged per submarket for ranking table comparison

## 6.5 Dev Capacity Extends the Time Horizon
- Standard supply analysis: S-02 + S-03 = 2-year window
- Dev Capacity Intelligence: DC-01 through DC-11 = 10-year window
- They work TOGETHER: S-outputs show near-term, DC-outputs show long-term
- Future Supply page MUST show both layers, not just one

## 6.6 Trade Areas Scope the Analysis
- MSA-level metrics can mask property-level reality
- TA-01 defines the competitive boundary
- TA-02 identifies who competes directly
- TA-03 recalculates supply-demand within that boundary
- Every property analysis should default to trade area scope, with MSA as benchmark
